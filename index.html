Environmental Impact

The planet pays
the real price.

Shein's own sustainability disclosures confirm what scientists have documented for years. These figures are not estimates — they are taken directly from the company's published reports.

Carbon Emissions

Emissions growing faster than revenue

26.2M
Metric tonnes CO₂e emitted in 2024

Shein's 2024 emissions rose 23.1% year-on-year — itself following an 81% increase from 2022. Upstream transport alone produced 8.52 million tonnes. Stand.earth ranked Shein the single largest polluting retailer in global fashion. Shein's net-zero pledge is 2050; at current trajectory, emissions are doubling every two years.

Shein 2024 Sustainability Report; Stand.earth Fashion Accountability Report 2024; Yale Climate Connections, Sept 2024
Water Usage

Thousands of litres for a single garment

2,700L
Fresh water to produce one cotton t-shirt

A single t-shirt requires 2,700 litres — enough drinking water for one person for 900 days. Jeans require up to 7,000 litres. The fashion industry uses 93 billion cubic metres of water annually. Textile dyeing is one of the world's largest sources of freshwater pollution, with chemical runoff contaminating rivers near factories.

European Parliament (2022); UN Environment Programme; Greenpeace Aotearoa
Microplastic Pollution

Invisible plastic in every ecosystem

81.5%
Of Shein's fabrics are synthetic polyester

Every wash of a synthetic garment releases hundreds of thousands of microplastic fibres. Fashion contributes 500,000 tonnes of microplastics to oceans per year through laundry — 35% of all ocean microplastics. These particles have been detected in human blood, lungs, and breast milk. Polyester takes hundreds of years to biodegrade and only 6% of Shein's polyester is recycled, compared to Adidas at 99% and H&M at 94%.

IUCN (2017); Shein 2024 Sustainability Report; UNEP; journal article on clothing brands' recycled polyester use; Greentimes
Shipping Model

47× the emissions of ocean freight — by design

47×
More CO₂ from air freight vs ocean freight per ton-mile

Shein ships individual parcels by air from China to exploit the EU's €150 customs exemption threshold — packages under this value enter duty-free. This makes air freight economically viable while generating 47 times more greenhouse gas per ton-mile than ocean freight. From July 2026, the EU is implementing a €3 fee on all previously exempt packages.

InTek Logistics (2023) Air Freight vs Ocean Freight; presentation research by Staunton, Devlin, Murphy, Heffernan (2025)
Textile Waste

A rubbish truck of clothes dumped every second

92M
Tonnes of textile waste globally per year

Of the 100 billion garments produced globally each year, 92 million tonnes end up in landfill. 87% of all clothing materials end up in incinerators or landfill — only 1% is recycled. Shein lists up to 10,000–20,000 new products per day, each at prices that make disposal after a handful of wears economically rational for the consumer.

In Ireland specifically, 40% of discarded clothing has never been worn. The Irish fast fashion habit is not just a personal choice — it is a national environmental emergency. Ireland overshot its first carbon budget by 10 million tonnes. Textile consumption contributes to that overshoot.

Earth.Org; Ellen MacArthur Foundation; European Environment Agency (2022); The Roundup.org on global textile waste
Labour & Human Rights

The workers behind
your purchase.

Multiple independent investigations from 2021 to 2025 document the same reality inside Shein's supplier network. The facts below are cited from original reporting, not secondhand.

"I work every day from 8 in the morning to 10:30 at night and take one day off each month. I can't afford any more days off because it costs too much."
Shein supplier factory worker, Guangzhou, China — Public Eye investigation (2023), corroborated BBC investigation January 2025

The BBC spent days in Panyu, Guangzhou — known as "Shein Village" — in January 2025, visiting ten factories and interviewing over 20 workers. What they found matched four years of prior investigations: routine 75-hour weeks, in direct violation of Chinese labour law and Shein's own supplier code.

75hrs
Documented working week (2021–2025) — Chinese labour law limits workers to 40 hours per week with a maximum of 36 hours' overtime per month. Shein's own supplier code caps at 60 hours.
€278
Basic monthly wage (2,400 yuan) at Shein supplier factories — the living wage in China is 6,512 yuan (~€802/month). Even with extensive overtime, many workers earn between 4,000–10,000 yuan.
71%
Of Shein's audited suppliers scored C or lower on Shein's own internal grading system in 2023, per Shein's own sustainability report.
2
Child labour violations found in supply chain audits — confirmed in Shein's 2023 Sustainability Report. Shein states these were remediated. Independent investigators have not been able to verify all supply chain conditions.
Note
Shein disputes some findings, citing third-party audits showing workers earn more than twice the local minimum wage. These averages do not account for documented working hours. Sources: Public Eye (2021, 2023); BBC Jan 2025; CNN Business May 2024; Shein 2023 & 2024 Sustainability Reports; Asia Floor Wage Alliance.
How They Keep You Buying

Shein doesn't sell clothes.
It engineers addiction.

Shein capitalises on System 1 thinking — the fast, impulsive decision-making system identified by Nobel-prize winning psychologist Daniel Kahneman. Every design choice is deliberate.

Fake Countdowns & Artificial Urgency

Time-limited deals with countdowns that reset on refresh. "Only 3 left" warnings that never resolve. Sitewide coupons with hour-long expiries. All engineered to prevent System 2 (rational) thinking — the kind that asks "do I actually need this?"

Influencer & TikTok Algorithm Exploitation

Shein sponsors #sheinhaul content at scale across TikTok and Instagram, targeting women under 30. It leverages TikTok's algorithm — which holds vast behavioural data — to serve new styles to exactly the people most likely to impulse purchase. AI predicts which styles will trend before they do.

Gamification & Data Harvesting

Spinning wheels, points systems, and daily check-in rewards that require personal data to unlock. The app is designed to be opened daily — not to browse, but to be rewarded. This data also feeds Shein's AI demand prediction engine, making its supply chain hyper-efficient at the cost of consumer autonomy.

Prices Engineered for Disposal

A €5 top is not an opportunity to save money — it is a price point that makes the item feel disposable. This is intentional. When clothes are cheap enough to throw away, consumers buy more, more often. Shein's business model requires volume, and volume requires disposability. Survey data from Irish students confirms price is the dominant reason for purchasing.

Social Conformity & FOMO

Conformity is particularly high among young Irish consumers. Trends spread rapidly, and Shein — with 600,000 styles and 10,000–20,000 new daily additions — ensures it can supply any trend the moment it emerges. The fear of being seen wearing "last week's style" is the emotional engine. One interviewee stated "not everyone can afford to buy from other brands."

"Shop Like a Billionaire" — Exploiting Aspiration

Temu's slogan directly targets the financial aspirations of young Irish consumers who, in a cost-of-living crisis, want to appear wealthy. 629,000 Irish people live below the poverty line. 7.4% cannot afford brand-new clothing. These retailers do not solve that problem — they profit from it while externalising the true costs onto the planet and workers.

Sources: Daniel Kahneman, "Thinking, Fast and Slow" (2011); presentation research by Staunton, Devlin, Murphy, Heffernan (2025); CSO Enforced Deprivation Report; Public Policy.ie

What You Can Do

Fashion doesn't have
to cost the earth.

The most sustainable garment is one that already exists. These are not sacrifices — they are better choices for you, for workers, and for the planet.

01

Buy secondhand first

Vinted, Depop, Oxfam, and local charity shops offer the same styles at comparable prices — without new production emissions. Survey data shows Vinted and vintage shops are already the preference for many Irish young people. A secondhand garment produces roughly 70% less CO₂ than a new equivalent.

02

Choose natural fibres when buying new

Organic cotton, linen, and hemp biodegrade and release far fewer microplastics. Look for GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard) or Oeko-Tex certification. Irish-stocked retailers like Penneys carry certified basics — the gap between ethical and affordable is smaller than Shein wants you to believe.

03

Use Good On You to check brands

The Good On You app rates brands on environmental, labour, and animal welfare criteria. Brands assessed by the Fashion Transparency Index or B Corp certification publish verified data on wages, emissions, and materials. Paying more for a garment made ethically is a direct act of accountability.

04

Wear what you already own

87% of Irish people agree it's important to buy clothes that last — yet 40% of discarded clothes in Ireland were never worn. Extending the active life of a garment by 9 months reduces its carbon, water, and waste footprint by 20–30% (WRAP). The opportunity cost of unused clothing is enormous.

05

Use a microplastic filter bag

Products like the Guppyfriend wash bag capture microplastic fibres during laundry cycles. Even one wash bag, used consistently, intercepts hundreds of thousands of fibres per wash before they reach rivers and oceans — and eventually human bodies.

06

Support buy-local, buy-Irish

When Irish consumers purchase from Chinese-owned companies, the money leaves the Irish economy without returning. Local retailers — including charity shops — support Irish jobs and reduce the trade deficit. The cost-of-living crisis is real, but Shein profits from it while worsening the conditions that cause it.

SHEIN'S MODEL ONLY WORKS WHILE WE FUND IT.

Every purchase is a vote. Withdrawing even some of those purchases — and sharing what you know — is the most direct accountability available to us.
